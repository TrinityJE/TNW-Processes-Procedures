Guiding Principles

Working regime, Office Presence & Holidays

  • We work 39.5 hours per week, and everyone should ensure his/her working hours are covered
  • As for working schedule, you can discuss this with your manager (different needs can be considered)
  • The official working regime is 3-2-3-2 working from home/working in the office (for variations such as 3-3-2-2, please discuss with your manager)
  • If you want to, you can come to the office even 4 or 5 days per week
  • Your office days are already registered in our team calendard (make sure you open the current year file). Ensure, you update it accordingly in case you work exceptionally from home (sickness, medical or other appoitment, etc.)
  • In the same callendar, register your wish holidays (w) and/or confirmed holidays (v)
  • Last but not least, you should also register working from abroad (a) in the same callendar. For more info about working from abroad below.
  • If public holidays or your holidays falls on your office day, you are not obliged to compensate for that day

Planned medical appointment

If possible, plan your medical appointment out of working hours. If not possible, you should:

  • Inform your colleagues (your team) when you are leaving for the appointment and when you are back (for urgent back-up matters)
  • Get a certificate from your doctor (for insurance purposes if something happens to you on the way to/from the doctor)
  • Ensure you compensate your working hours (a planned medical appointment is not part of your working hours)

Urgent medical appointment

If possible, try to get an appointment out of working hours. If not possible, you should:

  • Inform your colleagues (your team) when you are leaving for the appointment and when you are back (for urgent back-up matters)
  • Get a certificate from your doctor (for insurance purposes if something happens to you on the way to/from the doctor)

Sickness

If you feel sick (sore throat, coughing, temperature, digestion problems, etc.), please stay home to ensure that other members of the team are not contaminated. You should:

  • Inform your manager asap (before 9am) and let him/her know if you are up to working or not (to kick in back-up process if necessary)
  • At the same time, if possible, write a message to everyone on the 'General' Slack channel, so all team members are aware (as it might take some time before the manager is able to inform them)
  • If you feel this is not a one-day thing, try to book yourself an urgent medical appointment asap (only one day can be accepted without a medical certificate)
  • Based on your appointment:
    • If the doctor recommends a sick leave, take it!
    • If the doctor recommends working from home, ask him/her to confirm this in written if possible
  • Forward your medical certificate to your manager so this can be processes and booked in Attentia system
  • If you get sick at the office, you can leave home:
    • Inform your manager and let him/her know if you are up to working or not (to kick in back-up process if necessary)
    • Remember: only ONE DAY without a medical certificate can be accepted

Educational Leave

You might request an education leave if you are studying in one of the approved institutions by Belgian government. This leave is is an addition to your legal holiday and can only be taken during the school year period. Be sure that your attendance is in accordance with the required minimum, otherwise you will loose the right and might be fined if you already used some of the holidays. For further information, please discuss with your manager.


Working from abroad

You can work from abroad as per the rules below.

However, to be covered by insurance, when working from abroad, you have to send an email to your manager with the following info:

  • The dates you will be working from abroad
  • The address where you will be staying

The general rule is:

  • 10 days across the year
  • 2 working weeks during Chrismas holidays (last/fist week of the year, depending on when the 25/12 falls)

For any exceptions, please discuss with your manager.


Expense notes

The procedure is as follows:

  • Send your expense note in a correct format to ORN email
  • Expense notes are reviewed and approved once a month; if all is good, you will get a confirmation that your expense note will be paid
  • Once approved, it is sent to an accountant to pay it

Transport

We are all entitled to transport allowance:

  • Train – paid 80% by employer, 20% by SNCB; please contact your manager for further details
  • Metro/Tram/bus – paid 80% by employer; no more need for a proof of buying your subscription every month/3months, etc. It would be only needed if you are asking for this contribution for the first time
  • Bike/Car – a contribution is calculated based on number of km; please contract your manager for further details
